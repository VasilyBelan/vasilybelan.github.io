Scientists explained why you want to sleep in the rain

According to Quartz, chronobiologist Naomi Rogers from Central University of Queens has named several new reasons for reporters who, according to scientists, make a person sleepy in the rain. Firstly, the sun ceases to shine in the rain, blocking the production of the "night hormone" melatonin in the human body during the day. That is, the biological clock does not work on time.

Secondly, the smell of earth after rain is a calming effect on a person. As a rule, plants in dry periods emit special oils, presumably blocking seed growth in conditions of lack of water. Rain washes these oils, mixing them with geosmin, an organic substance produced by bacteria that is responsible for the earthy smell. The result is a new, distinctive smell with a relaxing effect.

The third reason may be ozone after a thunderstorm - a modification of oxygen, consisting of triatomic molecules. And finally, the rhythmic noise of the rain itself. According to scientists, the falling of raindrops creates the so-called pink noise. This is one of the background noise, the same as white noise, sounding in the range from 20 Hz to 20 thousand Hz. According to some reports, pink noise improves a person’s sleep, reducing brain activity. Scientists can’t confirm their conclusions with laboratory tests, but they suggested that the doubters do a personal experiment and sit for half an hour by the window, listening to the sound of rain.
